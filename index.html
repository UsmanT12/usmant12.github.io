Usman Tahir's Website

My name is Usman Tahir, and I am a Computer Science major at California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo (Cal Poly SLO). I’m passionate about building technology that creates meaningful impact and connects innovation with people.

My journey began at El Camino College, where I earned associate degrees in Mathematics and Physics along with a Certificate of Achievement in Computer Science, graduating with a 3.87 GPA. During my last year, I became apart of the inaugural cohort of the Sharer Scholars Program, a leadership and academic excellence initiative that supports high-achieving STEM students. I also worked as a student Front Desk Worker at the MESA (Mathematics, Engineering & Science Achievement) program for 2 years, supporting students, managing data, and strengthening the STEM community.

Through the Computing Talent Initiative (CTI) program, I expanded my technical skills by completing the CTI Accelerate Program and contributing to open-source software during a Google-sponsored micro-internship. Later, as a Software Engineering Intern at CodeDay, I developed a machine-learning image classifier using Python, PCA, and NumPy to explore data-driven visual recognition.

At Cal Poly SLO, I continue to grow as a Sharer Scholar and an active member of Hack4Impact, the Blockchain Club, and the CS AI Club. I also serve as a Junior Executive for the Middle Eastern and North African (MENA) Club, helping organize cultural and community-building events on campus. My current project, a WNBA Stats Tracker, combines my interests in sports analytics and software engineering by using Python, PostgreSQL, and React to analyze player performance data.

Outside of academics, I enjoy playing and watching basketball, exploring nature, and continuous learning. I’m motivated by curiosity and a genuine desire to make technology accessible and impactful.
